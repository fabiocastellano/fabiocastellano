Antonio Franca

Toni | BSc student at Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam

I am a Mathematician (in process) with the goal of understanding the nature of abstraction and developing algorithms capable of autonomous abstraction (i.e. general intelligence). I am very concerned about the future dangers that AI could entail for humanity, and I want to contribute to shape a safe future for Artificial Intelligence (why this is relevant). My current academic plan is to develop a strong mathematical background with the goal of transitioning, after gaining these quantitative capabilities, to Artificial Intelligence and Software.

At the time of writing, I consider two main global problems in which I intend to engage my efforts in order to make a positive contribution. The criteria I have used to decide to focus on these global pressing problems and not on others is based on weightings on the parameters: scale - neglectedness - solvability - personal fit. More about this criteria can be found here.

The first problem is, as I mentioned earlier, AI Alignment. Artificial intelligence has the potential to create enormous value for society, but as with any powerful technology, it comes with heightened risks and liabilities. We need to create a comprehensive consideration of how to manage the long-term risks of AI systems, including potential long-term hazards. How can we design powerful AI systems and be sure that they will do what we want, and not have unintended consequences? We need to actively guide AI in the right directions and practice proactive risk management if we want to usher in a better future. Because of this, I consider doing technical AI research as an effective way to mitigate certain types of existential risks that technological advancement can entail.

The second problem is Animal Welfare. As an adherent of utilitarianism (or utilitarian), I consider we should weigh the well-being of everyone equally, and this implies that we should make helping others a very significant part of our lives. Moreover, because of I consider well-being to be the most fundamental intrinsic value we should pursue when systemically making ethical schemes (or taxonomies), I think we should consider morally all sentient beings as they can suffer and perceive well-being. There are so many animals around the world suffering from being used for entertainment, food, medicine, fashion, scientific advancement, or as exotic pets, and besides that, it is an incredibly neglected problem. Because of this, I consider that working in animal welfare is a highly impactful matter to reduce globally suffering.

In my spare time, I love to travel and chat with new people, I enjoy camping in landscapes with breathtaking views, I love diving and the sea, and sports in general. Twice a year I get it in my head to be a powerlifter, or calisthenics athlete, but luckily (or not) it doesn't last long. Also, I like music a lot, I've been playing guitar for a while now, and I'm currently deepening my knowledge of flamenco rhythms with an Andalusian teacher. In the past I used to make songs and from time to time I remember those periods of life and I lock myself in the cave with the sole purpose of spending my time making music. If you would like to connect for whatever purpose, do not hesitate to do so, it will be my pleasure!
